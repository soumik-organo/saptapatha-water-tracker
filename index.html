📐 How Reference Values Are Derived — Antharam (Organo, Chevella) ▼ Show

All reference values come from actual measured data at Organo Antharam — 182 houses, 60 acres, Chevella taluk, Telangana. ⚠️ Important: Readings were captured at 20.9% occupancy — 79 villas handed over, 38 currently occupied (152 of 728 planned residents present). The planning LPCD of 150 is a chosen standard (IS 1172 basis). Actual measured LPCD = 810.9 (elevated due to fixed infrastructure shared by fewer residents). Full-occupancy demand ≈ 4.8× current actuals. The 5-Year Projection tab handles the ramp-up automatically.

Demand Benchmarks
Reference ValueAntharam FigureFormula / Source
LPCD 150 L/person/day Chosen planning standard (aligned with IS 1172 for group housing). Applied to design population (728) for full-occupancy projections.
Cross-check against measurement: 4.5 Cr L measured at 38 occupied villas (152 persons) → actual LPCD = 4,50,00,000 ÷ 152 ÷ 365 = 810.9 LPCD — elevated because fixed infrastructure (common areas, clubhouse, roads, pool) is shared across only 21% of residents. Planning LPCD of 150 is conservative and appropriate for full-occupancy design.
Domestic / House / Month 18,000 L/house/month = Planning LPCD × avg occupancy × 30 days = 150 × 4 × 30 = 18,000 L/house/month.
Cross-check against measurement: 4.5 Cr L ÷ 38 occupied houses ÷ 12 = 98,684 L/occupied-house/month — high because fixed costs (common areas, club, roads) are borne entirely by 38 households. At full occupancy (182 houses), fixed costs spread across all → per-house figure approaches the 18,000 planning standard.
Irrigation / Acre / Month 2,38,095 L/acre/month = Annual irrigation (2 Cr L) ÷ (12 months × 7 acres) = 2,00,00,000 ÷ 84 = 2,38,095. Validated against actual water meter on irrigation line.
Dripper Crosscheck Total 16,66,667 L/month = Annual irrigation (2 Cr L) ÷ 12 months = 16,66,667 L/month avg. This is the metered actual; dripper count × flow × time gives the bottom-up cross-check.
Swimming Pool Top-up 5,26,000 L/month Actual measured pool water consumption — accounts for evaporation losses (~3–5 mm/day), splash & backwash. Pool volume × turnover rate.
Other Consumption 83,555 L/month Residual metered consumption not classified under domestic, irrigation, or pool — includes community spaces, club house, roads washing, fire-fighting top-up.
Supply Benchmarks
Reference ValueAntharam FigureFormula / Source
RWH Storage per House 65,934 L/house = Total RWH storage capacity ÷ total houses = 1,20,00,000 L ÷ 182 houses. Used to scale project RWH storage proportionally.
RWH Total Storage 1,20,00,000 L (1.2 Cr L) Physical capacity of the Dugwell / sump at Antharam — engineered & constructed storage volume. Sets the ceiling on usable monthly RWH (each month's harvest is capped at this value).
RWH Monthly Supply (avg usable) 76,23,000 L/month = Σ min(monthly harvest, 1.2 Cr storage) ÷ 12. Total theoretical annual rainfall = 18 Cr L. Monthly harvest distributed by Chevella rainfall pattern — capped at 1.2 Cr L for Jun–Oct (monsoon months exceed tank capacity). Annual usable = 9.15 Cr L → monthly avg = 76.23 lakh L.

Seasonal Demand Factors DOM: ×0.85–1.2  |  IRR: ×0.5–1.3 Domestic factor peaks Mar–Jun (summer heat → ×1.2) and dips Jul–Sep (cooler monsoon → ×0.85). Irrigation factor peaks Apr–May (dry sowing → ×1.3) and drops Jul–Sep (rain-fed, minimal irrigation → ×0.5).
